Guide to Everything's Connected

What it Does

This app allows you to find all the artists that someone has collaborated with on Spotify. It uses the Spotify API to scan through all of an artist's songs and pull out every artist that was featured on them. You then have to ability to do the same thing to any those artists, and create a big network showing how a lot of artists have some connection to another. It's kinda like the famous Kevin Bacon Number thing. The only reason you should need to sign in is to add your followed artists. If you want to learn more about the app or myself you can look here.

Search for Artist or Playlist

Type any artist's name in the text box and press Enter or the search button. Their circle will appear, then it will do a search for all of their collaborators. Depending on how prolific the artist is, this could take a few seconds. For smaller artists, their name might get superseded by a more popular one. In that case you can also enter a link to an artist copied from Spotify. Finally, you can also enter a link to a playlist, and every artist in that playlist will be added and searched depending on the settings below.

Add Followed Artists

Requires signing in. Adds every artist you follow to the screen. By default none of them are expanded so you can try to connect them yourself. If you put on Full Search with one degree and don't Show Leaves, then you can see how your followed artists are directly connected.

Add Random Artist

Adds a random artist to the screen. They will always have connections and have some popularity. Try to keep adding random artists and seeing if you can connect them to your existing network.

Reset

Resets the whole graph and starts over. Don't tell anyone, but it just refreshed the page.

One at a Time Mode

This will only show the artists within 1 degree of separation from the artist you type, then you can click on any of those artists to run the same search on them to expand the network. This is my recommended mode since it runs faster and it's easier to see the information. There's also fun in discovering it yourself.

Full Search Mode

This will keep doing a recursive search on every artist found up to a certain degrees of separation. You can set the degree limit with the slider right below it. This can be cool to see the shear number of artists in the network, but it can take a while to load them all, that many artists causes massive lag, and it's hard to see what's going on when there's a couple thousand artists shown. I only recommend going to two degrees unless you know what you're doing or you're willing to wait a while.

Smart Filter

Filters out artists that have low popularity or have no image. This will help get rid of some unpopular artists that often times haven't even released their own music but are featured on a song.

Show Images

Shows images of the artist in their node. It looks better, but at a high volume it can cause extra lag. When this is off each node will be a color based on it's degrees of separation from the root.

Base Circle Size on Popularity

Spotify assigns every artist a popularity score between 0 and 100. With this setting on, the radius of each circle will be equal to that popularity. This can help you pick out the more famous artists. If you want equal representation then you can turn this off and each circle will be the same size.

Show Leaves

When this is unselected it will only show artists that have more than one connection. Useful for condensing the information to more relevant connections and reducing lag. This combos well with a Full Search on an artist or all followed artists to see how dense of a network they create.

Song connecting the artists

If you click on the line between two artists, it will show an embedded Spotify music player where you can listen to the song and go to the album page in Spotify.

Games You Can Play

I have fun putting it on the one at a time mode, and trying to find a way to connect two disparate artists. Using Random Artist helps for this You'd be amazed at some of the connections you can make with only a few degrees.

Theoretically with most artists you can go functionally forever expanding nodes until you reach a point where you have virtually every artist represented, but you're browser would probably crash before that happened. I want to put a challenge out there to find the artist with the largest network you can with no more connections to be found. This means expanding every artist until there's no more.

Kevin Bacon has appeared on a few movie sound tracks and released music with his brother as The Bacon Brothers. This means that you can use this app to find a musician's Bacon Number.
